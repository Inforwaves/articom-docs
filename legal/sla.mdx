## Service Level Agreement

This SLA applies to SaaS Services provided by Articom under an active Order Form.

## 1. Uptime Guarantee

### 98% Monthly Uptime
Articom guarantees that the SaaS Services will maintain an Uptime of at least **98.0%** in any given calendar month.

### Calculation
```
Uptime = (Total Minutes in Month - Downtime Minutes) / Total Minutes in Month

Example for 30-day month:
Total Minutes: 43,200
Maximum Allowed Downtime: 864 minutes (14.4 hours)
Uptime Target: 98%
```

### Exclusions
Downtime does NOT include unavailability due to:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Scheduled Maintenance">
    - Articom provides at least 48 hours notice for non-urgent maintenance
    - Scheduled during off-peak hours when possible
    - Emergency maintenance with reasonable notice
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Force Majeure Events">
    - Acts of God, war, terrorism, natural disasters
    - Events beyond Articom's reasonable control
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Customer-Side Issues">
    - Customer or End User equipment/software/network problems
    - Customer misuse of Services
    - Customer infrastructure failures
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Third-Party Service Outages">
    - Third-Party AI Services (Google Gemini, Azure OpenAI)
    - Telecom carriers and SIP trunking providers
    - Public network service disruptions
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## 2. Support Response Times

Technical support provided to designated customer contacts via:
- Email: support@articom.io
- Support Portal: dashboard.articom.io/support
- Enterprise: Dedicated Slack channel (Enterprise plans only)

### Priority Levels

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="P1 - Critical">
    **Definition**: System-wide outage; core functionality completely unavailable
    
    **Response Time Target**: 1 Hour
    
    **Examples**:
    - Platform completely down
    - All chatbots non-responsive
    - Voice agents not answering calls
    - Complete API failure
    
    **Support**: 24/7 monitoring and response
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="P2 - High">
    **Definition**: Critical component malfunctioning with significant business impact
    
    **Response Time Target**: 4 Hours
    
    **Examples**:
    - Major feature not working
    - Performance severely degraded
    - Integration failure
    - Data sync issues
    
    **Support**: Business hours with escalation available
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="P3 - Normal">
    **Definition**: General inquiries, bug reports, or performance degradation
    
    **Response Time Target**: 24 Business Hours
    
    **Examples**:
    - Minor bugs
    - Feature requests
    - Configuration questions
    - Documentation issues
    
    **Support**: Business hours (Monday-Friday, 9 AM - 6 PM)
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Response Time Definition
"Response Time" is measured from Articom's receipt of a properly submitted support ticket to when an Articom support engineer acknowledges the ticket and begins working on the issue.

### Business Hours
- **Standard Hours**: Monday-Friday, 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM (Customer's local timezone for Enterprise)
- **P1-Critical**: 24/7/365 monitoring and response
- **Holidays**: Reduced support except for P1 issues

## 3. Service Credits

### Eligibility
If Articom fails to meet the 98.0% Uptime guarantee in any calendar month, Customer is eligible for a service credit.

### Credit Amount
```
Service Credit = 4% of Monthly Subscription Fee
```

For the affected SaaS Service for that month.

### Example Calculation
```
Monthly Subscription: $1,000
Uptime Achieved: 97.2% (below 98% threshold)
Service Credit: $40 (4% of $1,000)
```

### How to Claim

<Steps>
  <Step title="Monitor Uptime">
    Track service uptime through Articom dashboard
  </Step>
  <Step title="Calculate Downtime">
    Verify downtime exceeds allowed threshold
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit Request">
    Email support@articom.io within 30 days of month end
  </Step>
  <Step title="Provide Details">
    Include month, downtime incidents, and supporting evidence
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receive Credit">
    Credit applied to next month's invoice
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Important Limitations

<Warning>
  Service credits are Customer's **sole and exclusive remedy** for any breach of this SLA.
</Warning>

- Must request within 30 days of month end
- Credit applied to future invoices only (no cash refunds)
- Maximum total credits: 100% of one month's subscription fee
- Credits don't extend subscription term

## 6. Support Best Practices

### For Faster Resolution

<Steps>
  <Step title="Provide Details">
    Include clear description, steps to reproduce, and impact
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign Priority">
    Use appropriate priority level based on business impact
  </Step>
  <Step title="Include Context">
    Screenshots, error messages, timestamps, affected users
  </Step>
  <Step title="Single Point of Contact">
    Designate primary contact for issue coordination
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Escalation Process

```plaintext
Support Ticket Created
        ↓
Initial Response (per SLA)
        ↓
Working Resolution
        ↓
[If not resolved within expected time]
        ↓
Escalation to Senior Engineer
        ↓
[If P1/P2 still unresolved]
        ↓
Escalation to Engineering Manager
        ↓
[Enterprise: If needed]
        ↓
Escalation to VP of Engineering
```

## 7. Maintenance Windows

### Scheduled Maintenance
- **Advance Notice**: Minimum 48 hours
- **Preferred Time**: Weekends or off-peak hours
- **Duration**: Typically under 2 hours
- **Impact**: Clearly communicated

### Emergency Maintenance
- **Notice**: As soon as reasonably possible
- **Justification**: Critical security or stability issues
- **Duration**: Minimized to extent possible
